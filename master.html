The Great Gatsby

by F. Scott Fitzgerald

A classic novel exploring themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream.

To Kill a Mockingbird

by Harper Lee

An enduring story of racial injustice and moral growth set in the American South.

Pride and Prejudice

by Jane Austen

A timeless romance novel depicting the societal norms and prejudices of 19th-century England.

The Catcher in the Rye

by J.D. Salinger

A coming-of-age novel following the adventures of Holden Caulfield in New York City.

The Hobbit

by J.R.R. Tolkien

An epic fantasy adventure about Bilbo Baggins' journey to reclaim the Dwarf Kingdom of Erebor.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

by J.K. Rowling

The first book in the Harry Potter series, chronicling the magical journey of a young wizard.

Brave New World

by Aldous Huxley

A dystopian novel exploring themes of technology, social control, and individual freedom.

Lord of the Flies

by William Golding

An allegorical novel portraying the descent into savagery of a group of British boys stranded on an uninhabited island.

The Alchemist

by Paulo Coelho

A philosophical novel about a young Andalusian shepherd named Santiago who journeys to Egypt in search of treasure.

Animal Farm

by George Orwell

A satirical allegory about a group of farm animals who rebel against their human farmer, only to establish a tyranny of their own.

Little Women

by Louisa May Alcott

A coming-of-age novel following the lives of four sisters—Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy—during the American Civil War.

The Picture of Dorian Gray

by Oscar Wilde

A philosophical novel about a young man named Dorian Gray, who remains youthful while a portrait of him ages.

Fahrenheit 451

by Ray Bradbury

A dystopian novel depicting a future society where books are banned and "firemen" burn any that are found.

Crime and Punishment

by Fyodor Dostoevsky

A psychological thriller exploring the moral dilemmas and consequences of a young student's decision to commit murder.
